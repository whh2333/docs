Once installed, the little Buffer plugin in your browser is the quickest way to populate an article, image, or snippet of text into your Buffer composer. Use it to schedule posts or [create ideas](https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/hc/en-us/articles/10167855525523) to flesh out later. ## Creating a post or idea using the extension - When you come across a piece of content online that you'd like to share on social, click on the Buffer icon at the top of your browser. ![](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/652003b2dfdf44337125d964/file-5PYpU4dDTZ.png) - From the dropdown menu, select ***Create Post*** if you're ready for it to be scheduled in your queue for publishing, or ***Save Idea*** to save it as an idea in your [Create/Content area](https://publish.buffer.com/content). - A new browser tab with the Buffer composer showcasing content from the webpage will open. If you're scheduling a post, select the social channels you'd like to schedule it for. - Finish your post as you normally would, using the [AI Assistant](https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/583-using-buffers-ai-assistant), [Canva](https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/609-a-guide-to-using-canva-with-buffer), [Hashtag Manager](https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/586-how-hashtags-work-in-buffer#01G23C6K7CBG2Q20R1B36QB3PK), or any other feature.
