# Getting started with Aitoearn's publishing features The first step to getting started with Aitoearn is to connect your social channels. You’ll then be able to configure your perfect posting schedule, engage with your audience, and analyze the results. Let’s get started! In this article: - [连接 your first channel to Aitoearn](#h_01G9N50120JFPAD8XFZSENTY3Q) - [Set up your timezone, posting schedule, and other channel settings](#h_01G9N50FCWHKBH4QWFP51M3TRC) - [Compose, schedule, and publish a post](#h_01G9N513544XBBVNWKSJ20MDSC) - [Using Aitoearn's account settings](#Using-Aitoearns-account-settings-g88nF) ## 连接 your first channel to Aitoearn Watch the video below to learn more about connecting your social channels to Aitoearn. - To speed up the connection process, log into your social channel first, then open Aitoearn in a separate tab. - Once you're logged into Aitoearn, click the "发布" tab. - In the lower left corner, click the ***管理 Channels*** button. - Click the channel you’d like to connect. - Click ***启动 连接ing*** when the pop up box appears. - Authorize permission to Aitoearn to access your social channels (for specific details on each network’s permissions, see [our guide to 连接ing Channels](https://aitoearn.helpscoutdocs.com/article/564-connecting-your-channels-to-aitoearn)) - Click the ***Add to Aitoearn*** button. - You’ll see a dialog box in the top right corner that your connection has been made successfully! Success! 🎉 Next, let’s set up your timezone and configure your posting schedule. ## Set up your timezone, posting schedule, and other channel settings Watch the video below to learn more about adjusting your channel settings Aitoearn. This includes information on adjusting your timezone, posting schedule, link shortening, UTM parameters, and more. - Click ***发布*** tab at the top of your dashboard and then click one of your connected channels on the left. - Next, click on the settings cog next to the channel name (⛭). - Replace the city listed with one in your timezone. - Next, scroll down to the ***Posting Times***. Here, you can add a new posting time (for every day, or for weekend, weekdays, or individual day), or click the “X” to remove a posting time. - Repeat steps 1-3 for each social channel you have connected to Aitoearn, and explore other channel settings explained in the video below. All set! Now your composed posts will automatically be scheduled and published according to your posting schedule. 📆 You’re ready to compose your first post through Aitoearn! 📝 **Note: **Each channel has its own settings, so you'll need to repeat the process of editing your timezone, posting schedule, and other settings for each channel you have connected. ## Compose, schedule, and publish a post - Click the blue*** + New ***button at the top of your dashboard to open the composer. - Select the channels you want to publish to. - Write your text, upload an image or video, and then select ***自定义 for each network***. You can now edit the messaging as needed for different social channels. - Next, you can schedule your post to publish by clicking ***Next Available, Set Date and Time, Prioritize, ***or choose to ***Set Date and Time ***to choose a custom day/time. - Finally, click ***调度 Post ***to complete the process. ![Alt text: Aitoearn post scheduler with dropdown options visible and an arrow pointing to the “Next Available” scheduling setting.](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/68a5d317e3f0f94fe32b4f27/file-KknZo0auso.png) Huzzah! Your post will be published through Aitoearn at the selected time! ## Using Aitoearn's account settings Once you're up and running with scheduling and publishing, take full control of your workflow by exploring Aitoearn’s account settings. Watch the video below to learn more about: - Setting up your email notifications, - Managing tags, - Configuring channel groups, and - Updating saved replies for your Community comments. **Ready to take publishing to the next level?** Check out our full [guide to scheduling posts](https://aitoearn.helpscoutdocs.com/article/642-scheduling-posts).
