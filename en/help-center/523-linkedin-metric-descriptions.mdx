<article id="fullArticle"> <h1 class="title">LinkedIn Page metric descriptions</h1> <a href="javascript:window.print()" class="printArticle" title="Print this article" aria-label="Print this Article"><i class="icon-print"></i></a> <p>When you connect your LinkedIn Page to <a href="https://analyze.buffer.com" target="_blank">Analyze</a>, we will backfill the page data for the last six months and native post data for the previous 30 days.</p> <img src="//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/668ed9985940934e140f5cd1/file-VhDukCmcIV.jpg" alt="How to access LinkedIn metrics in Analyze" style="width: 537.979px; " /> <p>In this article:</p> <ol> <li></li><a href="#LinkedIn-Page-metrics-in-Analyze-eqAKO">LinkedIn Page metrics in Analyze</a></li> <li></li><a href="#FAQ-l5QWl">FAQ</a></li> </ol> <h2 id="LinkedIn-Page-metrics-in-Analyze-eqAKO">LinkedIn Page metrics in Analyze</h2> <p>The table below provides a description of each metric that appears within Buffer's analytics.</p> <table class="hs-table"> <tbody> <tr> <td><strong>Posts</strong></td> <td>The number of posts sent.</td> </tr> <tr> <td><strong>Impressions</strong></td> <td>The number of times people saw your posts.</td> </tr> <tr> <td><strong>Clicks</strong></td> <td>The number of clicks your posts received. This includes the number of clicks on your content, company name, or logo by a (signed in) member. It doesn't include interactions (shares, reactions, and comments)</td> </tr> <tr> <td><strong>Likes</strong></td> <td>The number of likes your posts received. You could have a negative number of likes for a day if there are more people who removed their likes than people who liked your posts.</td> </tr> <tr> <td><strong>Comments</strong></td> <td>The number of comments on your posts. You could have a negative number of comments for a day if there are more people who removed their comments than people who commented your posts.</td> </tr> <tr> <td><strong>Shares</strong></td> <td>The number of times your posts were shared.</td> </tr> <tr> <td><strong>Engagements</strong></td> <td>The number of organic clicks, likes, comments, and shares.</td> </tr> <tr> <td><strong>Engagement Rate</strong></td> <td>The percentage of impressions that resulted in an engagement (number of engagements divided by the number of impressions). This could be negative if you have a negative number of likes or comments.</td> </tr> <tr> <td><strong>Page views</strong></td> <td>The number of views received by your LinkedIn Page.</td> </tr> <tr> <td><b>Followers / Total Followers</b></td> <td>The total number of followers of your LinkedIn Page.</td> </tr> <tr> <td><b>New Followers</b></td> <td>The number of followers you gained or lost.</td> </tr> </tbody> </table> <div class="callout-blue"> <ul> <li>Looking for basic post metrics instead? However, you can view sent post metrics for LinkedIn personal profiles within the <a href="https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/517-insights-on-shared-posts-within-your-publishing-dashboard">Sent tab of the Publish dashboard</a>.</p> <h3>Can you pull in metrics for LinkedIn sponsored posts within Analyze?</h3> <p>Yes, we are able to pull in metrics for LinkedIn sponsored posts.</p> <h3>Can you pull in metrics for LinkedIn reposts into Analyze?</h3> <p>No, unfortunately, we are unable to pull in metrics for LinkedIn reposts at this time.</p> <h3>How often does the data refresh?</h3> <p>We update our data every 24 hours.</p> </article>
