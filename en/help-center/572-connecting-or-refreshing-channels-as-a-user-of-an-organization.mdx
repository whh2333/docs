# Connecting or refreshing channels as a user of an organization <a href="javascript:window.print()" class="printArticle" title="Print this article" aria-label="Print this Article"><i class="icon-print"></i></a> <div class="callout-red"> <p>In order to connect or refresh channels as a user of an organization, your account will need to be granted Admin access within Buffer. [Learn more about user permissions and access here](https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/670-adding-users-and-setting-up-permissions-in-your-organization#h_01EJGFT20P5NVRX8N5QNMNNPHC).</p> </div> In this article: <li></li>[Choosing the right organization](#Choosing-the-right-organization-uDpX3)</li> <li></li>[Locked channels or losing access to Buffer tools](#01F8XC6QFBA57V77H1YP83AWWF)</li> <li></li>[FAQ](#FAQ-AsJDx)</li> ## Choosing the right organization <p>By default, you also have your own personal Buffer account with a personal organization. You may choose to connect your own channels to the publishing tool under the Buffer Free plan. In this case, when connecting or refreshing a channel, **it’s important to make sure you're working within the right organization**.</p> To see your organizations, click the drop arrow next to your profile avatar to open the menu. From there, select the correct organization. Once selected, your dashboard will refresh and those channels will now be accessible to you. ![A picture of the upper right Buffer dashboard showing an organization list](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/65149919f8eafb48f4e81327/img-1703224-1695848728-1002742817.png) <p>After you’ve selected the correct organization, you can now [connect](https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/564-connecting-your-channels-to-buffer) or [refresh](https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/573-refreshing-a-channel-in-buffer) the social channels.</p> ## Locked channels or losing access to Buffer tools <p>If you notice that some of the channels you have access to have been locked, this is likely because the owner of the organization downgraded their account: [What happens when you downgrade to the Free plan](https://buffer.helpscoutdocs.com/article/597-what-happens-when-you-downgrade-to-the-free-plan). When a plan is downgraded, the account is moved to the Free plan with access to only three social channels and no additional users. This is the reason those channels become locked for you.</p> In this case, you should chat with the organization owner and see if they had intentionally downgraded the account. It’s possible they didn’t know that downgrading their account would stop you from being able to access those channels or tools. ## FAQ ### Why can't I see my team's channels to refresh them? <p>If you've been invited to an organization, but can't see channels listed on the [Channels page](https://publish.buffer.com/all-channels):</p> - Check to make sure that you're viewing the correct organization [using these steps](#Choosing-the-right-organization-uDpX3).</li> - Check with your team to make sure that you have been granted Admin access of the organization. [Learn more about user permissions and access here](https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/670-adding-users-and-setting-up-permissions-in-your-organization#h_01EJGFT20P5NVRX8N5QNMNNPHC).</li> - If you're still having trouble, please reach out to our Customer Advocates at [hello@buffer.com](mailto:hello@buffer.com) and we'd love to help!</li>
