<article id="fullArticle"> <h1 class="title">Adding alt text to your images</h1> <a href="javascript:window.print()" class="printArticle" title="Print this article" aria-label="Print this Article"><i class="icon-print"></i></a> <p>Currently, it is possible to add alt text to static images scheduled to X/Twitter, Mastodon, Threads, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Facebook, Instagram, and Bluesky through Buffer.</p> <div class="callout-blue"> <p>üìù Looking for the mobile version of the <a href="https://buffer.helpscoutdocs.com/article/630-adding-alt-text-to-your-images-on-the-buffer-mobile-app" target="_blank">Adding alt text to your images</a> article?</p> </div> <p>To add alt text to your image, click on the image you uploaded within your Buffer composer. This will open the image in a larger view and there will be a text box at the bottom where you can add your alternative text description.</p> <p>While we recommend using less than 200 characters to align with <a href="https://www.accessibility.com/blog/what-are-best-practices-for-creating-alt-text">accessibility best practices</a>, the character counts for alt text in Buffer are as follows:</p> <ul> <li>Pinterest - 500 characters</li> <li>LinkedIn - 300 characters</li> <li>X/Twitter, Mastodon, Threads, Facebook, Bluesky, Instagram - 1,000 characters</li> </ul> <img src="//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/651499a98820396325b6fdd9/img-1662243-1695848872-1166508507.png" alt="Buffer composer image - Click to expand and add description pop-up" style="width: 100%; " /> <p>Once you're done, click <i><b>Save. </b></i></p> <img src="//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/651499a98820396325b6fdda/img-1662243-1695848872-1454554275.png" alt="Image of man with sunglasses and a text box to add alt text beneath it" style="width: 100%; " /> <div class="callout-blue"> <p><b>üìù Good to Knows</b></p> <ul> <li>It is only possible to view alt text on Instagram with a screen reader.</li> <li>Currently within Buffer, it is not possible to add alt text to:</li> <ul> <li>GIFS</li> <li>Google Business Profile posts</li> <li>Tiktok posts</li> </ul> </ul> <p>We are committed to improving in the area of accessibility, and you can find our full <a href="https://buffer.com/accessibility" target="_self">Accessibility Statement here.</a>&nbsp;</p> </div> </article>
