# How to delete posts in bulk <a href="javascript:window.print()" class="printArticle" title="Print this article" aria-label="Print this Article"><i class="icon-print"></i></a> If there's an issue with posting to one of your channels and several posts fail, you can remove your failed posts in bulk by using one of two methods. In this article: <li></li>[Clean Queue](#h_01GK221XS8YNJZZBR5RJX6VATQ)</li> <li></li>[Empty Queue](#h_01GK2224TNBKCWY063VZVGKY8M)</li> <div class="callout-blue"> <p>üìù Looking for the mobile version of the [How to delete failed posts in bulk article?](https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/623-how-to-delete-posts-in-bulk-in-the-buffer-mobile-app)</p> </div> ## Clean Queue <p>If you'd like to remove all of the failed posts in one of your queues, you can use Clean Queue. Clean Queue will only remove **failed posts** from your queue. It will *not* impact scheduled posts that have not failed. </p> To do this: - Open buffer.com. - Choose ***Channels ***from the left side panel.</li> - Select the channel where you'd like to remove the failed posts from. <li></li><p>Click the gear icon next to the channel name***.***</p>![Facebook queue with an arrow pointing to the channel settings (gear icon)](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/66b65fa765e01777a933364f/file-Gn3mjulR7w.jpg)</li> - Click ***General. ***</li> - Scroll down and click ***Clean Queue. ***</li> ![Buffer Clean Queue option](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/66b660db48915f570c6a932a/file-IU27riNhhu.jpg) - Click ***Clean Queue ***on the pop-up message to confirm.</li> ![Clean queue confirmation popup](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/651dc05542d97d1c04591478/file-WVfp4oV9pz.png) ## Empty Queue <p>If you'd like to delete **all **posts from your queue, including both failed posts and scheduled posts, you can use ***Empty Queue***. </p> To do this: - Open buffer.com. - Choose ***Channels ***from the left side panel. </li> - Select the channel where you'd like to empty the queue. <li></li><p>Click the gear icon next to the channel name***.***</p>![Buffer channel settings gear icon](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/66b65fa765e01777a933364f/file-Gn3mjulR7w.jpg)</li> - Click ***General. ***</li> - Scroll down and click ***Empty Queue. ***</li> ![Buffer channel settings - Empty queue button](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/66b661b0067e9c553dc82598/file-H4wymHOWFt.jpg) - Type the word 'Empty' to confirm and then click ***Empty Queue ***to complete the process.</li> ![Pop-up message asking to please type empty to proceed](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/683dcaf3db1d6854da33ea11/file-6ZnvuWRwzf.jpg)
