        <h1 class="title">Using Grammarly with Buffer</h1>
    
        <a href="javascript:window.print()" class="printArticle" title="Print this article" aria-label="Print this Article"><i class="icon-print"></i></a>
        <p>Previously, it was possible to use the Grammarly extension within the Buffer composer. Unfortunately, this recently stopped working due to some composer improvements that our team has made to support some of our newer features.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Our engineering team is aware of the issue and has this flagged to work on. While we don't have an estimate for when it might be fixed, as a workaround, you can&nbsp;<a href="https://www.grammarly.com/desktop">use the desktop version of Grammarly</a>, which works just fine with Buffer.&nbsp;</p>
