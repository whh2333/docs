        <h1 class="title">How to delete posts in bulk on the Buffer mobile app</h1>
    
        <a href="javascript:window.print()" class="printArticle" title="Print this article" aria-label="Print this Article"><i class="icon-print"></i></a>
        <p>If there's an issue with posting to one of your channels and several posts fail, you can remove your failed posts in bulk by using one of two methods.</p>
<p>In this article:&nbsp;</p>
<ol>
 <li><a href="#h_01HC02N9FNKJYT0GDQWQNSTWWG" target="_self">Clean Queue</a></li>
 <li><a href="#h_01HC02N9FN2SJECPSW02RM9CT6" target="_self">Empty Queue</a></li>
</ol>

<h2 id="h_01HC02N9FNKJYT0GDQWQNSTWWG">Clean Queue</h2>
<p>If you'd like to remove all of the failed posts in one of your queues, you can use Clean Queue. Note that Clean Queue will not impact scheduled posts.</p>
<ol>
 <li>Open your Buffer mobile app and tap the menu icon in the upper left of your screen.</li>
 <li>Tap the social channel where you'd like to remove the failed posts.</li>
 <li>Tap <em><strong>Settings</strong></em> in the bottom right corner of the app.</li>
 <li>Tap <em><strong>Clean Queue</strong></em>.</li>
 <li>Tap <em><strong>Yes</strong></em> to confirm.</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="h_01HC02N9FN2SJECPSW02RM9CT6">Empty Queue</h2>
<p>If you'd like to remove all posts from your queue, including both failed posts and scheduled posts, you can use Empty Queue.</p>
<ol>
 <li>Follow steps 1-3 above.</li>
 <li>Tap <em><strong>Empty Queue</strong></em>.</li>
 <li>Tap <em><strong>Yes</strong></em> to confirm.</li>
</ol>
