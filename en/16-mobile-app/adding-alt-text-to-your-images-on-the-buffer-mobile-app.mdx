        <h1 class="title">Adding alt text to your images on the Buffer mobile app</h1>
    
        <a href="javascript:window.print()" class="printArticle" title="Print this article" aria-label="Print this Article"><i class="icon-print"></i></a>
        <p>Currently, it is possible to add alt text to static images scheduled to X/Twitter, Mastodon, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Threads, and Facebook through Buffer.</p>
<div class="callout-blue">
 <p>üìù Looking for the web browser version of the "<a href="https://buffer.helpscoutdocs.com/article/618-adding-alt-text-to-your-images" target="_self">Adding alt text to images</a>" article?</p>
</div>
<p>When you have added an image to your post in the Buffer composer (<a href="https://buffer.helpscoutdocs.com/article/632-scheduling-posts-on-the-buffer-mobile-app" target="_self">Learn more about scheduling posts on the Buffer mobile app</a>), tap <strong><em>Add Description </em></strong>to add your alt text and then tap <strong><em>Done</em></strong> to save the changes. From there, you can finish scheduling your post.</p>
<p>While we recommend using less than 200 characters to align with <a href="https://www.accessibility.com/blog/what-are-best-practices-for-creating-alt-text">accessibility best practices</a>, the character counts for alt text in Buffer are as follows:</p>
<ul>
 <li>Pinterest - 500 characters</li>
 <li>LinkedIn - 300 characters</li>
 <li>X/Twitter, Mastodon, Threads, Facebook, Bluesky, Instagram - 1,000 characters</li>
</ul>

 <figcaption>

 </figcaption>

<div class="callout-blue">
 <p>üìù <b>Good to Knows</b></p>
 <ul>
  <li>It is only possible to view alt text on Instagram with a screen reader.</li>
  <li>Currently within Buffer, it is not possible to add alt text to:</li>
  <ul>
   <li>GIFS</li>
   <li>Google Business Profile posts</li>
   <li>Tiktok posts</li>
  </ul>
 </ul>
 <p>We are committed to improving in the area of accessibility, and you can find our full <a href="https://buffer.com/accessibility" target="_self">Accessibility Statement here.</a>&nbsp;</p>
</div>
