        <h1 class="title">Facebook metric descriptions</h1>
    
        <a href="javascript:window.print()" class="printArticle" title="Print this article" aria-label="Print this Article"><i class="icon-print"></i></a>
        <p>When you connect your Facebook account to <a href="https://analyze.buffer.com" target="_blank">Analyze</a>, we will backfill the page data for the last six months and native post data for the previous 30 days.</p>
<p><img alt="How to access Facebook metrics in Analyze" src="//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/64cb6cf86d4a3e191f237e66/images/668edaa7e1989867dceff0e7/file-Lp2kemaWau.jpg" style="width: 602.979px; max-width: 100%; "></p>
<p>The table below describes each metric that appears within Buffer's analytics. All metrics are pulled from Facebook's Graph API.</p>
<div class="callout-blue">
 <ul>
  <li>Looking for basic post metrics instead? Check out our guide to <a href="https://support.buffer.com//support.buffer.com/article/517-insights-on-shared-posts-within-your-publishing-dashboard">Insights on sent posts within the Publish dashboard</a>.</li>
  <li>Seeing conflicting information? Checkout the article: <a href="https://buffer.helpscoutdocs.com/article/525-why-your-data-in-buffer-might-differ-from-your-native-analytics" target="_self">Why your data in Buffer might differ from your native analytics</a></li>
 </ul>
</div>
<table>
 <tbody>
  <tr>
   <td><strong>Posts</strong></td>
   <td>The number of posts sent.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
   <td><strong>Page &amp; Post Engagements</strong></td>
   <td>The number of times people have engaged with your posts through likes, comments and shares and more.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
   <td><strong>Engagement Rate</strong></td>
   <td>The percentage of impressions that resulted in an engagement (total number of comments, clicks, shares and reactions divided by the number of impressions). This is organic data only (organic clicks and organic impressions). Paid metrics are not included.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
   <td><strong>Reactions</strong></td>
   <td>The number of reactions your posts received.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
   <td><strong>Shares</strong></td>
   <td>The number of times your posts were shared.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
   <td><strong>Comments</strong></td>
   <td>The number of comments on your posts.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
   <td><strong>Post Clicks</strong></td>
   <td>The number of clicks your posts received. This number includes clicks on the post, links, images or videos and multiple clicks by one user. It includes any link that takes a user away from Facebook.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
   <td><strong>New Fans</strong></td>
   <td>The number of fans you gained or lost. Fans are people who have specifically "liked" your page. Fans does not include followers.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
   <td><strong>Total Fans</strong></td>
   <td>Your total number of fans.&nbsp;Fans are people who have specifically "liked" your page. Fans does not include followers.</td>
  </tr>
 </tbody>
</table>
<div class="callout-blue">
 <p>üìù Due to recent updates from Meta, we are no longer able to ingest the following data for Facebook Pages:</p>
 <ul>
  <li>number of post clicks</li>
  <li>organic impressions</li>
  <li>engaged users</li>
  <li>gender or age demographics.</li>
 </ul>
 <p>However, paid data is not impacted by this change and we are still able to ingest paid impressions, paid reach, and paid link clicks.</p>
</div>
